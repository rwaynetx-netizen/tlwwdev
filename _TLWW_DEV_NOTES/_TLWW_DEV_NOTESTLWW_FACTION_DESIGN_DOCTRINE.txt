TLWW DEVELOPMENT NOTE
Faction Design Doctrine

Date Logged: 2026
Author: TLWW Development

Purpose
Define the guiding principles for alliance formation and faction behavior in TLWW in order to prevent unrealistic ideological megablocs and reinforce the mod’s geopolitical realism.

---

I. PROBLEM WITH VANILLA HOI4 FACTIONS

In vanilla Hearts of Iron IV, factions tend to form along purely ideological lines.

Typical examples include:

All fascist states aligning into a single bloc

All communist states forming a global alliance

Democratic states forming a unified coalition

While this simplifies gameplay, it produces unrealistic geopolitical outcomes.

Historically and in the TLWW setting, ideological similarity alone does not guarantee alliance compatibility.

States with similar ideologies may still compete due to:

Civilizational differences
Strategic rivalries
Regional interests

Therefore TLWW should avoid allowing ideological megablocs to form automatically.

---

II. TLWW DESIGN PRINCIPLE

Faction formation should be determined by a combination of factors rather than ideology alone.

Primary determinants:

Ideological compatibility
Civilizational alignment
Strategic threat environment
Regional interests

Ideology influences cooperation but does not guarantee alliance.

---

III. EXPECTED FACTION TYPES

Rather than a small number of ideological super-blocs, TLWW should produce several competing geopolitical coalitions.

Examples include:

Atlantic Coalition

Likely members

United States
United Kingdom
Canada
Poland
Australia

Primary focus

Preserving Atlantic strategic dominance.

---

Multipolar Strategic Bloc

Likely members

Russia
China
Various regional partners

Primary focus

Opposition to Atlantic geopolitical dominance.

---

Regional Civilizational Alliances

These alliances may form between culturally aligned states rather than ideologically identical ones.

Examples

European Restoration Pact
Islamic Sovereignty League
Pacific Security Compact

These alliances may change over time depending on world events.

---

IV. ROLE OF NATIONAL INTEGRALISM

National Integralism should not automatically produce a unified global alliance.

Instead it should create multiple civilizational nationalist movements.

Examples

Western Integralist states

United States
Parts of Europe

Islamic Integralist states

Iran
Potential regional partners

Asian Integralist states

Japan
Possible regional allies

These movements may cooperate temporarily but are not guaranteed long-term allies.

---

V. DIGITALISM AS A BRIDGE

Digitalism is a unique ideology in the TLWW system because it is trans-civilizational.

Digitalist actors prioritize technological sovereignty and algorithmic governance rather than cultural identity.

This allows Digitalist coalitions to form between otherwise incompatible states.

However such coalitions should generate internal political tension.

Example

Atlantic-Digital coalition within the United States.

---

VI. IMPLEMENTATION APPROACH

Faction formation should be controlled primarily through:

Focus tree decisions
Diplomatic events
Strategic threat triggers
AI alliance weighting

Rather than automatic ideology-based faction creation.

This allows dynamic coalition building depending on world events.

---

VII. DESIGN OBJECTIVE

The diplomatic system should produce a world where:

Multiple competing alliances exist simultaneously

States shift alignments depending on strategic pressure

Civilizational conflicts shape long-term geopolitical blocs

This structure reinforces TLWW’s central narrative premise.

The Last World War is not simply ideological.

It is a struggle between competing visions of civilization.

---

END OF NOTE
